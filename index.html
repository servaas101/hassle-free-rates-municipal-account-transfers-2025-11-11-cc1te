Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our municipal account services

Most property transfers are completed within 3-4 weeks from the time we receive all necessary documentation. The timeline depends on the municipality's processing speed and whether any complications arise. We maintain constant communication with municipal authorities to expedite the process where possible. In some cases, we've completed transfers in as little as 10 business days. We provide regular updates so you always know where your application stands.

Generally, we require the following documents: proof of property ownership (title deed), identification documents for both the transferor and transferee, the municipal account number and current bill, proof of residence, and any relevant power of attorney documents if applicable. Our team will provide you with a complete checklist tailored to your specific situation. We handle all document verification and submission to ensure nothing is missed. If you're missing any documents, we can often assist in obtaining them through the appropriate channels.

Our dispute resolution process begins with a thorough investigation of your billing history. We analyze consumption patterns, compare your bills with similar properties, and identify any discrepancies or errors. Once we've gathered evidence, we prepare a formal dispute application with supporting documentation and submit it to the municipality. We then follow up regularly to ensure your case receives proper attention. Our success rate in resolving billing disputes is over 85%, and we often help clients recover significant overcharges. We keep you informed throughout the entire process and represent your interests professionally.

Outstanding charges can complicate transfers, but we have extensive experience handling these situations. We work with municipalities to establish payment plans, negotiate settlements, or in some cases, dispute incorrect charges. Depending on the circumstances, we may recommend settling the debt before transfer, arranging a payment plan, or having the new owner assume responsibility. We advise you of all options and their implications so you can make an informed decision. Our goal is to find a solution that allows your transfer to proceed without unnecessary delays or financial burden.

We believe in complete transparency. Our fees vary depending on the complexity of your case and the services required. A simple transfer typically costs between R1,500-R3,000, while more complex cases involving disputes or multiple issues may cost more. We provide a detailed quote upfront before starting work, and there are no hidden costs. Our fees cover all professional services, document preparation, municipal liaison, and follow-up communication. We also handle all administrative costs associated with the process. If your case takes longer than expected due to municipal delays, we don't charge additional fees—we're committed to seeing it through to completion.
